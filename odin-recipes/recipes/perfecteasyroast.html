The Roast in Question

If you have ever had a dry tough roast, you understand the frustration and desire to find something, any recipe, to satisfy the qualities of a tender, juicy, flavorful roast! Why waste your money on a piece of meat or other form of protein just to consume an unsatisfactory outcome. Among other proteins, I have found that utilizing a smoker to cook roast has been the absolute, most satisfying version.

Yield 2 Servings

It is recommended that you find a good quality meat ingredient for best results, but the use of this method produces a far more satisfactory result, resulting in a lower end meat becoming almost unrecognizeable, and pushing high quality meats to new levels of flavor and texture.

Ingredients

Meat Slab

  • Roast of choice, option to supplement with a steak but will require variations of alternate temperature and time.

Au Jus

  • onion [2]
  • salt [to cover slab]
  • olive oil [3 tbs]
  • seed mustard [1tsp]
  • horseradish [1tsp]

Recipe

  1. Cover meat with generous amount of salt, refrigerate overnight
  2. Prepare smoker , raise heat to 250 degrees
  3. Remember to soak woodchips before beginning to smoke
  4. Tending to smoke levels intermitently, let meat heat and smoke for about an hour and a half per pound, alternatively it is recommended to check internal temperature of meat and assure its doneness
  5. When ready, remove from smoker and keep any escaped juice in a pot and place on stove on low for sauce
  6. Sear on stove until exterior gains dark golden brown slightly crispy texture
  7. Let rest beneath foil for 20-30 minutes
  8. While meat rests, add remaining Au Jus ingredients to saucepan on stove and simmer for 10 minutes
  9. Slice meat 1/3 inch thick or to your liking
  10. Enjoy an incredibly delicious roast!
